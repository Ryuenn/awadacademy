About Awad Academy

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about who the program is for, how trainings work, and what to expect from Awad Academy's trial advocacy education.

Awad Academy is built for personal injury attorneys, trial lawyers, and legal professionals who want to sharpen courtroom strategy, jury communication, and case preparation skills. Aspiring advocates and law students looking for real trial frameworks can also benefit from the program.
The Academy offers both live, one-time intensives and a structured program experience. Live trainings focus on deep-dive sessions like the Exclusive Trial Strategy Dinner, while the broader program provides layered learning across preparation, jury engagement, and persuasion tools.
Yes. Every session is centered on practical frameworks, language patterns, and trial tools that Ibrahim Awad uses in real verdicts. You'll leave with concrete models you can adapt for openings, examinations, and negotiations.
Awad Academy is an educational resource, not a law firm. Nothing provided in the program or trainings is legal advice, and participation does not create an attorney-client relationship. Individuals needing legal advice should consult a licensed attorney in their jurisdiction.
Trainings are offered in-person and through live virtual sessions, with select insights available on-demand. Each format focuses on discussion, real case examples, and practical application rather than lecture-only teaching.

Upcoming live events and programs are shared on the site and through direct email updates. If you'd like early access to new trainings, use the contact page to let us know you want to be added to the interest list.
